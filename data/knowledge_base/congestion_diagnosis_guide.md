# Congestion Diagnosis Guide

This is a sample traffic knowledge base document for demos and interviews.

## Common Causes
Congestion can be caused by low speed, high flow, high occupancy, incidents, roadwork, weather, signal faults or bottleneck capacity limits.

## Low-Speed Congestion
Low speed with high occupancy usually indicates queueing or spillback. Check upstream and downstream segments.

## High-Flow Congestion
High flow with moderate speed may represent peak demand. It requires monitoring rather than immediate intervention.

## Incident-Induced Congestion
Accidents, illegal parking and signal faults can cause sudden congestion. Query traffic events before concluding cause.

## Bottleneck Segments
Ramps, lane drops and signalized intersections can form recurrent bottlenecks.

## Recurring vs Sudden Congestion
Recurring congestion appears during morning or evening peaks. Sudden congestion should be cross-checked with events and forecasts.
